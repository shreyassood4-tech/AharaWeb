If you follow a vegetarian or plant-based diet, there's one nutrient you almost certainly don't get enough of — and it might be affecting you right now without you knowing.

Vitamin B12 is essential for your nervous system, your red blood cells, and your DNA synthesis. Without it: fatigue, brain fog, nerve damage, mood changes. The problem — your body doesn't make B12 on its own, and it almost exclusively comes from animal products.

## Why Vegetarians Are at Risk

B12 is found in meaningful quantities in meat, fish, eggs, and dairy. While eggs and dairy contain some, amounts are often insufficient for daily needs. Studies show up to 92% of vegans have insufficient B12 levels, and a significant proportion of vegetarians fall into the same category.

The deficiency can be silent for years. B12 is stored in the liver, so symptoms may not appear until stores are critically low — by which point neurological damage may already be underway.

## The Absorption Problem

Your body needs a protein called "intrinsic factor," produced in the stomach, to absorb B12. Many people produce less of it over time — meaning dosage alone doesn't tell the full story.

Even if you take a high-dose B12 supplement, if your gut environment isn't supporting intrinsic factor production and B12 uptake, much of that dose passes through without being absorbed.
