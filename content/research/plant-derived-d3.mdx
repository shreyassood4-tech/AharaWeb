Check the label of any D3 supplement and you'll see "cholecalciferol." What it won't say is that for most supplements, this comes from lanolin — extracted from sheep's wool — making it non-vegetarian.

For anyone following a vegetarian or vegan diet, this is a hidden problem. You're taking a supplement you believe aligns with your values, and it doesn't.

## What Is Lichen-Derived D3?

Lichen are composite organisms found on rocks and trees across the world. They're one of the only non-animal sources of true D3 (cholecalciferol). Lichen-derived D3 is chemically identical to what your skin produces when exposed to sunlight. Not a compromise — the real thing.

## Why D3 Over D2?

Many plant-based supplements sidestep the lichen-vs-lanolin question entirely by using D2 (ergocalciferol) instead. But research is clear: D3 is roughly 87% more effective than D2 at raising and maintaining serum vitamin D levels, according to research published in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition.

If you're supplementing to correct a deficiency, D2 may not get you there — or may require significantly higher doses to achieve the same outcome.
